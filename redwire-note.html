Redwire Corporation: Aerospace Valuation Under Macro / Liquidity Stress

Redwire Corporation (NYSE: RDW) is a US space-infrastructure and defence-technology company whose 2025 acquisition-led expansion increased exposure to national-security demand while also amplifying operating losses, cash burn, purchased intangibles, goodwill and refinancing sensitivity.

02 · Economic value added

The company is destroying economic value on the FY2025 evidence.

Invested capital equals total debt of $88.4 million plus shareholders’ equity of $1,060.0 million, producing $1,148.4 million. Using a 7.60% aerospace-and-defence WACC and an 11.58% sector tax rate, estimated return on capital is −17.7%. EVA therefore equals approximately −$290.3 million.

A one-percentage-point increase in WACC would reduce EVA by approximately $11.5 million, making the investment thesis explicitly sensitive to rates and liquidity conditions.

03 · Principal risks

Three red flags determine the downside case.

1

Cash-flow quality and liquidity stress

Operating cash flow deteriorated from −$17.3 million in FY2024 to −$177.3 million in FY2025, driven by working-capital cash use and lower deferred revenue. Durable revenue growth therefore requires materially stronger cash conversion.

2

Acquisition accounting, goodwill and impairment

Goodwill increased to $779.1 million and intangible assets to $336.2 million. Together they exceeded shareholders’ equity of $1,060.0 million, while the same year included a $34.7 million impairment charge.

3

Contract concentration and revenue-estimate risk

Sixty-six percent of FY2025 revenue was recognised over time, and two customers each represented more than 10% of revenue. This creates milestone, timing and government-budget concentration risk.

04 · Recommendation

SELL over 12 months

Redwire has strategically attractive exposure to space infrastructure, national security and defence-technology demand. However, the financial-statement evidence does not yet support a buy: ROE is negative, EVA is materially negative and operating cash flow remains deeply adverse. Until EBIT margin and cash conversion turn positive, the equity is better treated as a liquidity-stressed optionality trade than as a compounding investment.
