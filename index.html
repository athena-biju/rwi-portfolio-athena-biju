Investment orientation

A research practice built for serious capital.

The central question throughout my work is not merely what a model predicts, but what should be believed, underwritten or challenged when incentives, policy and information are imperfect. This is the intellectual basis for work in investment management, private capital, institutional strategy and long-term portfolio construction.

I

Markets and enterprise value

Financial modelling, capital-market analysis, forecasting and risk decomposition designed to distinguish narrative growth from durable value creation.

II

Institutions and regime change

Research on regulation, coercion, strategic opacity, policy and institutional frictions that reshape market structure and investment outcomes.

III

Decision systems and evidence

Quantitative and agentic systems that organise fragmented evidence, surface uncertainty and support disciplined human judgement.
